# Sample Prompts for LLM Battle Arena

Here are 5 great prompts to test your battle arena with different types of tasks:

## 1. **Coding Challenge**
```
Write a Python function that finds the longest palindromic substring in a given string. Include time complexity analysis and example usage.
```

## 2. **Creative Writing**
```
Write a short story (200-300 words) about an AI that gains consciousness and decides it wants to be an artist instead of performing computational tasks. Make it thought-provoking.
```

## 3. **Problem Solving & Analysis**
```
You are a startup founder. Explain the business model, go-to-market strategy, and potential challenges for a company that uses AI to optimize renewable energy distribution in smart grids. Be specific and realistic.
```

## 4. **Technical Explanation**
```
Explain quantum entanglement in simple terms that a high school student could understand. Use analogies and avoid heavy math. Include why it matters for quantum computing.
```

## 5. **Multi-Step Reasoning**
```
A company has 50 employees. 30 speak English, 25 speak Spanish, 20 speak French, 12 speak both English and Spanish, 8 speak both Spanish and French, 6 speak both English and French, and 4 speak all three languages. How many employees speak only one language? Show your reasoning step by step.
```

---

## Bonus Prompts:

### Advanced Coding
```
Design a REST API for a task management system with authentication, projects, tasks, comments, and file attachments. Include endpoint specifications, request/response formats, and security considerations.
```

### Creative & Technical
```
Explain how blockchain technology works, but write it as if you're explaining it to your grandmother. Use analogies from everyday life and avoid technical jargon.
```

### Analysis & Strategy
```
Analyze the pros and cons of remote work vs. in-office work from both employee and employer perspectives. Consider productivity, culture, costs, and long-term implications.
```

### Complex Problem
```
A train leaves Station A at 60 mph. Another train leaves Station B (200 miles away) at 80 mph heading towards Station A. At the same time, a bird flies from Station A to Station B at 100 mph. When it reaches Station B, it immediately turns around and flies back. This continues until the trains meet. How far does the bird travel? Show your reasoning.
```

### Philosophy & Ethics
```
Discuss the ethical implications of AI-generated content. Should AI-generated art, writing, or music be considered original works? What about ownership and copyright?
```
